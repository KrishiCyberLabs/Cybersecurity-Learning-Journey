# 🌐 What is Networking?

## Overview

Before diving into topics like penetration testing or network security, it's essential to understand how devices communicate and exchange data. This room helped me build that foundation by explaining how networks work, how devices are identified, and how data travels between systems.

## What I Learned

A computer network is simply a group of devices connected together so they can communicate and share information. These devices can include computers, mobile phones, printers, servers, and many other types of hardware. Networks allow users to share files, access the internet, and communicate efficiently.

I also learned that the Internet is a massive network made up of millions of smaller networks connected together. Whenever we visit a website, send an email, or watch a video online, our devices are constantly communicating with other systems across the Internet.

Another important concept was IP addressing. Every device connected to a network has an IP address that acts like its digital identity. Without IP addresses, devices would not know where to send or receive data. Understanding IP addresses is an important step toward learning network scanning, routing, and penetration testing.

The room also introduced ICMP, a protocol mainly used to check whether a device is reachable on a network. I practiced using the `ping` command, which sends ICMP Echo Requests to another device and waits for a response. This is one of the simplest but most useful commands for checking network connectivity and troubleshooting communication issues.

Finally, I learned about Local Area Networks (LANs), which connect devices within a limited area such as a home, office, or school. Devices on the same LAN can communicate quickly and share resources without relying on the Internet.
